How big data sets are reasonable to load in d3 visualizations? What types of visualizations does a limit like that exclude? Give examples of visualizations that must be based on large data sets and discuss how they deal with this. Could you have reduced the size of the data sets in this assignment? (Tobi)

When making visualisation on the web, one must not only keep in mind the experience of the user, but also how much data can be displayed in a browser / loading times. In cases of much data, which would be necessary for huge maps displaying different aspects of the weather data on a daily or even more frequent basis to smoothly display changes over multiple years, one might face technical issues. In such cases one must reflect how detailed the information has got to be to portray relevant information in enough detail. It may be sufficient to display fewer years or decrease the frequency without reducing the information content massively. We have used yearly averages and thus dropped a lot of data for visualisation, which may have made our visualisation easier to understand, but also removed quite a lot of information. Further reductions don't seem reasonable. Another way than using less data is to reduce page loading times when working with D3 through pre-rendering, i.e. shifting part of the work from the client-side to the server-side.
